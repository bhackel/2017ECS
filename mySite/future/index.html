Neural Networks

The future will likely be mostly recurrent neural networks, but convolutional neural networks also have their pros. Convolutional neural networks are generally better at finding long term patterns while RNNs are better suited for small details. I expect a type of RNN in the future that can both do long term patterns and small details to arise.

Recurrent Neural Networks

Recurrent neural networks are in widespread use by Google with Google DeepMind, and TensorFlow by the Google Brain Team. Google only started using RNNs in mid-2012 with Android's speech recogniser. Even though RNNs are a few decades old, they are still relatively new compared to something like a convolutional neural network.

Neuroevolutional Networks

Although neuroevolution goes back all the way to the start of life on this planet, it may help us learn how life formed. Since neuroevolution depends on random chance to get to a goal, there is no predicting what a simulated cell could become. We do not currently have enough computational power in one place to do this, but we will likely in the future with quantum computing on the rise.
